03 · Flash path

Bootloader & flashing

This is your no-soldering door into the device. Two buttons and a USB cable put the SP-1 into a 60-second window where it will accept firmware. Master this before you build anything — it's also your recovery path when a bad build won't boot.

The trigger

▶ Enter the bootloader
  1. Make sure the SP-1 is off (or long-press •• to turn it off).
  2. Hold Track 1 + Track 4 while connecting USB-C.
  3. Keep holding until the Track 1 LED lights up.
  4. Release all buttons.
  5. The device is now a USB CDC ACM serial port, ready to flash.

The power button is not part of the combo — an old post claimed a "step 4: power button" that was later edited out. It's just the two track buttons plus USB-C. The device stays in bootloader mode for about 60 seconds of inactivity before rebooting into the app.

Three ways to flash

1 · The web flasher (recommended)

Since 2026-05-18, solderless.engineering exposes a dedicated firmware utility — a browser-based flasher (Web Serial, Chromium-family browsers). This is the first publicly-released browser-based custom-firmware flasher for the SP-1.

Open the launcher

Visit the site in Chrome/Edge and click connect; select "stem player."

Enter the bootloader

Hold Track 1 + Track 4, plug in USB-C, wait for the Track 1 LED, release.

Drop your binary

Switch to the firmware-utility tab, drag your zephyr.bin, click flash. No Python, no DFU tool.

✦ Source

Local mirror at assets/solderless-2026-05-18/utility/. The clean flash-only flow lives in utility/js/firmware.js.

2 · Your own client (when the web tool is offline)

The flash protocol is fully documented — anyone can implement it. The site went offline for nine days in May 2026, so plan for it being unavailable. theunflappable's test_bootloader.py (Discord #firmware, 2026-05-08) is a Python starting point. The byte-level details are on the next page. → The wire protocol

3 · SWD (for serious development)

If you solder SWD pads (there's no factory header), ninja -C build flash pushes the binary directly and you get breakpoints. This is the only path that survives overwriting the bootloader. Most people skip it until they're iterating hard.

▲ A bad firmware can brick the device

Your build can leave the device unusable if it doesn't bring up USB, doesn't honor the function-button long-press for shutdown, or disables the audio system with no recovery state. Recovery: re-enter Track 1+4+USB-C from a cold start — the bootloader is intact as long as you never wrote below 0x20000. tkt1000: "If you load a bad firmware you can brick your device!" The solderless UI warns: "the binary you upload must be compiled specifically for SP-1 or it might brick your device!"

✓ Golden habit

Don't flash anything onto your only device "for fun." sp1-midi turns your stem player into a MIDI controller and you can't undo it without re-flashing something. Develop against a unit you can afford to brick, or be very sure of your recovery path.
