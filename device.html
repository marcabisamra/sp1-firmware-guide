01 · Hardware

The device

Before you write a line of firmware, learn what's on the board. The whole project lives or dies on knowing which pin does what and where your code is allowed to live in flash.

What it is

The Teenage Engineering SP-1 (model Stem Player SP-01) is a handheld four-stem audio player, designed by TE for distribution alongside Kanye West's Donda and Jesus Is King. It was never sold at retail; units reached the public through gifts, leaks and resale. Internally it plays 4 stereo stems at 24-bit / 48 kHz with DSP effects and tape-style fast-forward/rewind.

◆ The nRF radio is dead weight

The TKT wiki states plainly: "there is no antenna connected to the MCU on the stem player PCB." All Bluetooth goes through the CYBT module over UART. Do not enable CONFIG_BT_LL_SOFTDEVICE or any Zephyr controller mode that drives the nRF radio — there's nothing to radiate from. TKT wiki: Hardware-overview, 2026-05-12

Flash memory map — where your firmware lives

This is the single most important table for a firmware author. The nRF's internal flash is split into a reserved bootloader and an application slot. Your code goes in the app slot. Never write below 0x20000.

PartitionOffsetSizePurpose
bootloader0x00000000128 KBTE bootloader. The Track 1+4 trigger lives here. Reserved.
image-0 (app)0x00020000892 KBApplication firmware — stock TE or your custom build.
storage0x000ff0004 KBZephyr settings (in the sp1-midi BSP). Page 0xFF reserved.
▲ Bricking boundary

If your firmware extends below FLASH_START = 0x20000 you overwrite the bootloader and lose the two-button recovery path. Recovery then requires SWD with mass-erase — opening the device and soldering. The 892 KB app slot is enormous (sp1-midi uses ~168 KB); you will not run out by accident. references/15-bootloader-protocol.md — Flash layout constants

The eMMC is separate

Two different storage chips, two independent operations:

  • nRF internal flash (1 MB) — holds firmware. Flashed via the bootloader.
  • eMMC (4 GB) — holds the audio album image (~311 MB). Written via a separate upload path.

Loading custom stems and loading custom firmware are unrelated. You can put your own music on a stock device without touching firmware at all (the stock TE firmware plays any correctly-formatted album). And the eMMC is not encrypted — just formatted strangely (8192-byte logical sectors of 16 native 512-byte blocks). Discord #hardware, tkt1000, 2026-05-07

How the buttons work (and why Track 1+4 is special)

The SP-1 reads its buttons through analog resistor ladders, not individual GPIOs. The Track and Play buttons share one ADC channel (AIN0) with 8 voltage thresholds each — enough to distinguish multi-button combinations. This is exactly why Track 1 + Track 4 can be detected as a distinct combo and used as the bootloader trigger. The volume/transport rocker is a second ladder (AIN1); the Function button is the one true discrete GPIO (P0.27, active-low).

✓ What to take away

Your firmware lives in the 892 KB app slot at 0x20000. It talks to codecs over I²C, streams audio over I²S, reads the eMMC bit-banged, reads faders/buttons over the ADC, and drives LEDs over PWM. The BSP gives you working drivers for all of this — your job is to orchestrate them.
